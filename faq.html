Frequently Asked Questions

1. What product ranges do you offer?
ALLURE HOUSEWARE is currently offering four distinctive cookware ranges under the brand TOPST TURVY:
  • Stainless Steel: Durable and resistant to rust and corrosion, perfect for everyday cooking. TOPSY TURVY Stainless steel cookware is renowned for its durability, performance and sleek appearance. Crafted from high-quality stainless steel, this cookware resists rust, stains and corrosion, ensuring long-lasting use in the kitchen. Thick encapsulated sandwich (SAS) bottom, with an aluminum core ensures superior heat conductivity and even cooking.
  • PVD Coating: TOPSY TURVY Stainless steel cookware with PVD (Physical Vapor Deposition) coating combines the strength of stainless steel with advanced technology for enhanced performance and aesthetics. PVD coating involves applying a thin, durable layer of metal, such as titanium or ceramic, onto the stainless steel surface through a vacuum process. This coating significantly improves the cookware's resistance to scratches, stains, and tarnishing, ensuring it maintains its sleek, modern appearance over time.
  • Hammered PVD Coating: TOPSY TURVY Stainless steel cookware with hammered PVD coating offers a unique blend of durability and aesthetic appeal. The hammered texture, achieved through a specialized process, provides not only a distinctive, elegant look but also enhances the cookware's heat distribution and cooking efficiency. The PVD (Physical Vapor Deposition) coating, applied over the hammered surface, adds an extra layer of protection, making the cookware highly resistant to scratches, stains, and tarnishing.
  • Black PVD: TOPSY TURVY Stainless steel cookware with black PVD (Physical Vapor Deposition) coating offers a sophisticated blend of durability and style. The black PVD coating provides a sleek & modern look while significantly enhancing the cookware’s resistance to scratches, stains, and tarnishing. This coating also imparts improved non-stick properties, making for easier food release and cleanup without relying on traditional chemical coatings.

7. How should I care for my cookware?
To maintain the quality and longevity of your cookware:

  • For PVD Coating: Hand wash and avoid metal utensils on the outside to prevent scratching.
  • For Hammered PVD and Black PVD: Similar care as PVD; always use soft sponges and avoid high heat.
